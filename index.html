WAYS TO PREVENT YOUR SLIDING GLASS DOOR FROM WRECKING YOUR HOME

We all know that dreaded sound of a glass door slamming shut. It’s usually followed by a scramble to find the house key, unlock the door and try to prevent as much damage as possible. But what can you do to prevent your sliding glass door from wrecking your home in the first place? Here are some tips!

Regularly clean your sliding glass door to prevent dirt and dust from building up

Cleaning sliding glass doors regularly is one way to prevent them from wrecking your home. If dirt and dust build-up, the door will be more likely to stick or not slide properly. This can cause the door to scrape against the frame of the house, potentially damaging both the door and the frame. In addition, if dirt and dust get into the track of the door, it can make it difficult for the door to move at all.

To clean a sliding glass door, you will need a bucket of soapy water, a sponge or rag, and a squeegee. Wet down the entire surface of the door with soapy water, then scrub off any dirt or grime with a sponge or rag. Be sure to get into the crevices where the door meets the frame. Once you have scrubbed the door clean, use a squeegee to remove all of the soapy water. Finish by drying the door with a clean towel.

Apply a sealant or lubricant to the tracks to keep them moving smoothly

The reason for this is that seals protect components from dirt and moisture buildup as well as keep them moving smoothly so they do not break or jam-like often happens with sliding glass doors when nothing is protecting them from wear-and-tear over time.

To apply a sealant, you need some petroleum jelly (Vaseline) and a clean cloth. Simply apply the petroleum jelly to the tracks using the cloth and then wipe away any excess so that it does not attract dirt. You can also use a commercial sealant or lubricant made specifically for this purpose if you prefer.

Be sure to do this regularly, at least once a month, to prevent damage from happening in the first place. It only takes a few minutes and is well worth it to keep your sliding glass doors functioning properly for years to come.
